Bio‑based wall technology – explanation for clients

This wall system is a fully bio‑based, vapour‑open construction that combines excellent thermal performance with healthy indoor climate and a natural appearance. It is designed as a timber‑frame wall filled with hemp‑lime insulation and finished with natural panels and a charred wood façade.

Layer build‑up (from inside to outside)

Interior finish – strawboard panel (30 mm)
A rigid board made from compressed straw fibres. It provides a smooth interior surface, adds some thermal insulation and acts as an additional moisture‑buffering layer inside the room. Typical thermal conductivity is around 0.09 W/m·K.

Insulation – hemp‑lime (160 mm)
The main insulating layer consists of a cast or sprayed mixture of hemp shiv and lime binder. Hemp‑lime has a low thermal conductivity (approx. 0.07 W/m·K) and forms a continuous insulation layer around the timber studs, reducing thermal bridges. It also has high thermal mass and moisture‑buffering capacity, which helps to stabilise indoor temperature and humidity.

Timber frame – 160 mm structural studs
A standard softwood frame that carries the structural loads of the building. The cavities between studs are completely filled with hemp‑lime, so the structure is integrated into the insulation zone.

Membrane – vapour‑permeable wind‑tight layer
On the cold side of the insulation there is a vapour‑open membrane. It protects the wall from wind and external moisture while still allowing water vapour to move through the assembly, so the wall can dry out in both directions.

Counter battens – 25×50 mm
Timber battens create a ventilated cavity between the membrane and the outer cladding. This air gap allows any moisture that reaches the back of the cladding to evaporate and also improves the durability of the facade.

Ventilated air cavity – 25 mm
A continuous, ventilated air space behind the cladding that drains water and equalises pressure, further protecting the wall from driving rain.

Façade cladding – charred wood (20 mm, Shou Sugi Ban)
The external finish is charred wood boards made with the traditional Shou Sugi Ban technique. The charred surface gives the facade a deep, rich appearance and significantly improves weather resistance and durability without synthetic coatings. Thermal conductivity is around 0.13–0.15 W/m·K, so the cladding also contributes a small amount of insulation.

Thermal performance

Based on the indicated layer thicknesses and material properties, the wall achieves an approximate thermal resistance of R ≈ 4.1 m²K/W, which corresponds to a U‑value of about 0.24 W/m²K. This means the wall has very good insulation performance and is suitable for low‑energy buildings under Central European climate conditions. A lower U‑value means less heat loss through the wall.

Key benefits for the client

Energy efficient – U ≈ 0.24 W/m²K reduces heating and cooling demand compared to standard constructions.

Healthy and vapour‑open – all main layers (strawboard, hemp‑lime, membrane, timber and charred wood) are vapour‑permeable, allowing the wall to regulate indoor humidity and avoid trapped moisture.

Low‑carbon and natural – materials are plant‑based (straw, hemp, timber) and lime‑based, with significantly lower embodied CO₂ than conventional mineral wool and concrete systems.

Durable facade – the charred wood cladding provides long‑lasting protection against weather while keeping a natural appearance.

Comfortable indoor climate – hemp‑lime and strawboard combine insulation with thermal mass and moisture buffering, leading to stable temperatures, reduced overheating and comfortable humidity levels.
